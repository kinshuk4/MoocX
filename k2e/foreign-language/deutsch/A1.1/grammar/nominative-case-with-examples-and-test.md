### Nominative Case

There are four cases in the German language. Nominative, Accusative, Dative and Genitive. The cases are an important part of the German grammar as they are responsible for the endings of adjectives, indefinite articles and when to use which personal pronoun. The nominative case is used for a person/animal/ thing which is doing the action. In this case, you will be able to ask: Who or what did/is something? Nominative is always used after the verbs sein and werden.
